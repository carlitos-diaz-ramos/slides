Homogeneous hypersurfaces in $\C P^n$ and $\C H^n$

[Takagi] A homogeneous hypersurface in $\C P^n$ is a principal orbit of the quotient of the isotropy representation of a Hermitian symmetric space of rank two.

[Berndt, Tamaru] Homogeneous hypersurfaces in $\C H^n$:

  • tubes around totally geodesic $\C H^k$, $k\in\{0,\ldots,n-1\}$
  • tubes around totally geodesic $\R H^n$
  • horospheres
  • ruled homogeneous minimal Lohnherr hypersurfaces $W^{2n-1}$, or their equidistant hypersurfaces
  • tubes around ruled homogeneous minimal Berndt-Brück submanifolds $W^{2n-k}_\varphi,$ for $k\in\{2,\ldots,n-1\}$, $\varphi\in(0,\pi/2]$
    ($k$ even if $\varphi\neq\pi/2$)

Homogeneous hypersurfaces in $\C H^n$

Hopf examples

  • Tubes around a totally geodesic $\C H^k$, $k\in\{0,\ldots,n-1\}$

    Group action: $S(U(1,k)\times U(n-k))$

    $g=2$ if $k\in\{0,n-1\}$;   $g=3$ otherwise

  • Tubes around a totally geodesic $\R H^n$

    Group action: $SO^0(1,n)$

    $g=2$ if $r=\log(2+\sqrt{3})$;   $g=3$ otherwise

  • Horospheres

    Group action: $N$

    $g=2$

Isoparametric hypersurfaces in $\C H^n$

[Díaz-Ramos, Domínguez-Vázquez, Sanmartín-López] Isoparametric hypersurfaces in $\C H^n$:

  • tubes around totally geodesic $\C H^k$, $k\in\{0,\ldots,n-1\}$
  • tubes around totally geodesic $\R H^n$
  • horospheres
  • ruled homogeneous minimal Lohnherr hypersurfaces $W^{2n-1}_{\pi/2}$, or their equidistant hypersurfaces
  • tubes around a ruled homogeneous minimal Berndt-Brück submanifolds $W^{2n-k}_\varphi$, for $k\in\{2,\ldots,n-1\}$, $\varphi\in(0,\pi/2]$
    ($k$ even if $\varphi\neq\pi/2$)
  • tubes around ruled homogeneous minimal submanifolds $W_{\g{w}}$, for some proper real subspace $\g{w}$ of $\g{g}_\alpha\cong\C^{n-1}$ such that $\g{w}^\perp$ has nonconstant Kähler angle

If $M$ is a connected complete hypersurface in $\C H^2$ then the following statements are equivalent:

  • $M$ is homogeneous
  • $M$ has constant principal curvatures
  • $M$ is isoparametric

If $M$ is a connected complete hypersurface in $\C H^n$ then, $M$ is homogeneous if and only if $M$ is isoparametric and has constant principal curvatures

Polar actions on real space forms

  • Spheres $S^n$:

  • Euclidean spaces $\R^n$

  • Real hyperbolic spaces $\R H^n$

Polar actions on $\C H^n$

[Podestà, Thorbergsson] A polar action on $\C P^n$ is orbit equivalent to the a quotient of an isotropy representation of a Hermitian symmetric space

[-, Domínguez-Vázquez, Kollross] Polar actions on $\C H^n$:

  • $\g{h}=\g{q}\oplus \g{so}(1,k)$, $k\in\{0,\ldots,n\}$
    $\g{q}$ subalgebra of $\g{u}(n-k)$
    $Q$ acts polarly on $\C^{n-k}$ with totally real section
  • $\g{h}=\g{q}\oplus\g{b}\oplus\g{w}\oplus\g{g}_{2\alpha}$
    $\g{b}$ linear subspace of $\g{a}$, $\g{w}$ real subspace of $\g{g}_\alpha$,
    $\g{q}$ subalgebra of $\g{k}_0=\g{n}_K(\g{a})$, $\g{q}$ normalizes $\g{w}$,
    $Q$ acts polarly on $\g{g}_\alpha\ominus\g{w}$ with totally real section

Recall: $\g{w}=\bigoplus_{\varphi\in\Phi}\g{w}_\varphi$
